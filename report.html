Observed Risk Implications



HTTPS Transport

Where HTTPS is present and correctly enforced, data exchanged between users
and the website benefits from encryption in transit. This reduces exposure
to interception, manipulation, or passive monitoring of network traffic.




HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS)

Where HSTS is not observed, browsers may permit fallback to unencrypted
connections under certain conditions. This increases exposure to downgrade
and interception scenarios on hostile or misconfigured networks.




Content Security Policy (CSP)

The absence of a CSP limits a site’s ability to explicitly control trusted
content sources. This increases susceptibility to script injection,
unexpected third-party execution, and supply-chain driven compromise.




Clickjacking Protection

Weak or missing clickjacking controls may allow a site to be embedded within
external frames. In some scenarios, this can be leveraged to manipulate user
actions without clear visibility or intent.




MIME Sniffing Protection

Without explicit MIME handling controls, browsers may reinterpret content
types. This increases the risk of unintended script execution when content
is misclassified or manipulated.




Referrer Policy

An undefined referrer policy may result in internal URLs or contextual data
being shared externally during navigation, increasing unintended information
exposure.




Permissions Policy

Where no permissions policy is observed, browser feature access defaults to
broader allowances. This increases reliance on browser behaviour rather than
explicit limitation of sensitive capabilities.




Assessment Integrity & Disclosure



This report is based on automated analysis of publicly observable website
behaviour at the time of assessment. It evaluates surface-level configuration
indicators only and does not perform penetration testing, exploit
development, vulnerability scanning, or active probing.




Findings do not confirm exploitability, unauthorised access, compromise, or
breach. Results are time-specific and may change as configurations,
infrastructure, or third-party services are updated.
